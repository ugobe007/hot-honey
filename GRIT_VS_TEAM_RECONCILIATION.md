## The Core Question

**"GRIT is all about founders, so how does it relate to startup scoring?"**

---

## üéØ The Key Distinction

### **TEAM Score** = What Founders **HAVE** (Static Attributes)
- Credentials (Stanford, MIT, Harvard)
- Experience (Ex-Google, Ex-Meta)
- Previous exits
- Domain expertise
- Education background
- **These are RESUME items** - what they've done in the past

### **GRIT Score** = What Founders **DO** (Dynamic Behaviors)
- Pivot history (how they adapt)
- Customer feedback frequency (how obsessed they are)
- Shipping velocity (how fast they execute)
- Iteration speed (how quickly they learn)
- **These are BEHAVIORAL indicators** - what they're doing NOW

---

## üîó The Reconciliation: How Founder GRIT ‚Üí Startup Quality

### Philosophy:
**"Founders with GRIT build better startups"**

GRIT is a **founder trait** that manifests as **startup behaviors** we can measure:

| Founder GRIT Trait | Startup Behavior We Measure | Startup Outcome |
|-------------------|---------------------------|----------------|
| **Persistence** | Pivot history (1-2 smart pivots) | Adaptability, learning |
| **Customer Obsession** | Daily/weekly customer feedback | Product-market fit |
| **Execution Speed** | Features shipped, deployment frequency | Velocity, momentum |
| **Resilience** | Overcoming obstacles, learned from failure | Long-term success |
| **Determination** | Time to iterate, shipping velocity | Competitive advantage |


## üí° Why This Matters

### The VC Reality:

1. **Credentials open doors** (Team score)
   - Ex-Google founder gets VC meetings
   - Stanford grad gets YC interviews
   - But credentials ‚â† success

2. **GRIT predicts success** (GRIT score)
   - Customer-obsessed founders build better products
   - Fast iterators find PMF faster

3. **The Best Founders Have Both**
   - Strong credentials (Team) + High GRIT = Unicorn potential

   - Strong credentials + Low GRIT = Risky (might not execute)

## üîç How We Measure GRIT at the Startup Level

Even though GRIT is a founder trait, we measure it through **startup behaviors**:

### 1. **Pivot History** (0-0.5 points)
- **Founder trait**: Adaptability, learning from mistakes
- **Startup behavior**: Number of pivots, pivot reasons
- **Why it matters**: Smart pivots = learning, not failure

### 2. **Customer Feedback Frequency** (0-0.5 points)
- **Founder trait**: Customer obsession
- **Startup behavior**: Daily/weekly/monthly customer contact
- **Why it matters**: Obsessed founders talk to customers constantly

### 3. **Time to Iterate** (0-0.5 points)
- **Founder trait**: Execution speed
- **Startup behavior**: Days from feedback to shipped update
- **Why it matters**: Fast iterators learn faster, win faster

### 4. **GRIT Signal Keywords** (0-6 bonus points)
- **Founder trait**: Persistence, resilience, determination
- **Startup behavior**: Keywords in descriptions ("customer obsessed", "shipped", "pivoted")
- **Why it matters**: Language reveals founder mindset

## ‚úÖ The Answer

**GRIT is about founders, but we measure it through startup behaviors because:**

1. **Founder traits manifest as startup actions**
   - Customer-obsessed founders ‚Üí daily customer feedback
   - Fast executors ‚Üí weekly shipping
   - Persistent founders ‚Üí smart pivots

2. **Startup behaviors are measurable**
   - We can't directly measure "grit" as a personality trait
   - But we CAN measure pivot history, shipping frequency, customer contact

3. **GRIT behaviors predict startup success**
   - More than credentials do
   - That's why GRIT has higher weight (5) than credentials (3)

4. **They're complementary, not redundant**
   - Team = "Can they get meetings?" (door opener)
   - GRIT = "Will they succeed?" (success predictor)

---

## üéØ Bottom Line

**GRIT is founder-focused, but measured through startup behaviors because:**
- Founder traits ‚Üí Startup behaviors ‚Üí Startup outcomes
- We can't measure personality directly
- But we CAN measure what gritty founders do
- And those behaviors predict success better than credentials

**The reconciliation: GRIT is the behavioral manifestation of founder traits that we measure at the startup level to predict success.**
